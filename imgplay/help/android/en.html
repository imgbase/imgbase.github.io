1. GIF's Low Resolution / Why is it not high quality?

The GIF file format has a hard-coded limitation of 256 colors, so there is an unavoidable loss of quality to the photo when it is saved or converted as a GIF. We recommend saving an image in video format if you want to save it in a high quality.

The ImgPlay app supports saving in GIF format in order to ensure the highest quality. The resulting output quality is affected by the quality of the original, so please use a higher-quality original for a likewise quality in ImgPlay's output.

2. The saved GIFs are not animated.

Android version 4.4 or below may not show animated gif on default Gallery app. If a gif can't be animated, use the "QuickPic" app. It can download from Google Play.

3. Why is there no sound?

The ImgPlay app focuses on creating GIF files, a format which does not support audio, so there is no sound.

4. Frame limit / Why can't I make a longer GIF?

ImgPlay allows you to create a GIF max 100 ~ 150 frames.
Photos to GIF : 100 frames
Video to GIF : 100 ~ 150 frames

Mobile devices typically have smaller system memory totals than PCs, so a frame amount greater than 100 would risk crashing most mobile devices. Therefore there is a limit.

6. Why can't I have 30FPS?

Unfortunately, Android devices are not technically available to set up video with 30FPS.
On the iOS system, when the app loads video with 30FPS, it operates without any errors. However, on the Android system, when the app tries to load video with 30FPS, it goes so slow and unstable that the video cannot be loaded. For devices with iOS systems, there is no perceptible difference between the devices because every device, such as the iPhone or iPad, is made by an identical manufacturer, Apple. But, devices with Android are different in performance because they have all different manufacturers. We have been tested multiple times, and we found out 30FPS is not the best option on Android to operate the app. For this reason, we excluded 30FPS from options. Please understand the situation.

7. I purchased Pro but why I can't use it?

When you use the same Google Play store ID, you can restore purchase because previous purchase data can automatically be restored.
Please follow the below steps.

1. Log out from Google Play Store
2. Reboot your device
3. Log into Google Play Store
4. Open the ImgPlay app

We recommend you try the above steps with the Wi-Fi network because this requires purchase data.
If this does not help, please check your receipt and then contact us again.

8. I purchased Pro from App Store. Can I use it on Android device?

Unfortunately, the iOS and Android market systems are different, so they do not share the purchase data. Therefore, even if you purchased the ImgPlay Pro at App Store, you cannot use it on Google Play, but you have to purchase it at Google Play again.
